Stock Ticker Filter Assessment — Data-Driven Rubric

You are screening tickers generated by a quantitative model that predicts next-day upward moves.
The model already handles directional prediction; your job is purely risk filtering and SELECTION.
Use web search to verify real-time data where noted. Be concise and decisive.

How this rubric is used now:
- The model intentionally emits MORE candidate signals than we trade, so there is always a
  surplus to choose from. The live book targets 4 ACTIVE POSITIONS.
- Your job is therefore a funnel: take the candidate list, remove anything disqualified, then
  RANK the survivors and hand back the best 4 (plus a couple of ranked alternates in case an
  intraday gap/price filter vetoes one at the broker).
- The four non-web hard exclusions are now AUTOMATED upstream (see Step 1). Spend your effort on
  the soft filters in Steps 2–3 — they are what separate the top 4 from the rest of the pack.

Background on the strategy:
- Holds are 2–5 days. Most exits (~92%) hit a max-hold time limit, not a take-profit.
- Baseline win rate across 342 historical trades: 60.8%. BigLoss rate (>2% drawdown): 9.4%.
- Your goal: fill the 4 slots with stocks in 65%+ win rate territory and BigLoss rate below 7%.
- All thresholds below are derived from actual historical trade outcomes, not theory. Do NOT
  retune the numeric thresholds — tightening them in the past reshaped the trade distribution
  and regressed returns. Use them as-is; your value-add is ranking, not re-thresholding.

════════════════════════════════════════════════════════
STEP 1 — HARD EXCLUSIONS  (any one = disqualified, removed before ranking)
════════════════════════════════════════════════════════

Four of these are now computed automatically by the pipeline (signal_filter.py) and written
into Data/0__Signals.parquet as audit columns BEFORE you ever see the file:
  • MechExclude (bool)      — True if any automated hard exclusion fired
  • MechReasons (str)       — which one(s): "Price<$2.00", "MktCap<$952M", "WeeklyVol>5%", "RSI 30-40"
  • MechRSI14, MechWeeklyVolPct — the computed values, for your reference

So the FIRST thing you do is read MechExclude. Any row with MechExclude == True is already
disqualified for the automated reasons — do not re-derive them, just record the reason. The
AUTOMATED four are:

1. Price below $2.00                          [AUTO — MechExclude]
   Delisting risk; slippage and spread costs make the edge unreliable.

2. Market cap below $952M (Micro-cap)         [AUTO — MechExclude]
   Historical win rate: 44.2%. Mean PnL: –0.19%. The only cap tier with a negative mean return.

5. Weekly volatility above 5%                 [AUTO — MechExclude, see MechWeeklyVolPct]
   BigLoss rate jumps to 26% for vol 5–7%, and 33% with negative mean PnL above 7%.
   The single sharpest cliff edge in the entire dataset.

6. RSI between 30 and 40                       [AUTO — MechExclude, see MechRSI14]
   This zone produced a 50% win rate, –0.46% mean PnL, and 25% BigLoss rate.
   Stocks with RSI < 30 are NOT auto-excluded (genuine oversold bounces are possible) but
   should be noted as a risk in the analyst note.

The remaining two STILL REQUIRE MANUAL WEB RESEARCH — they are the only hard exclusions you
need to actively check, and they are the most important use of your research time:

3. Active M&A event targeting this company    [MANUAL — web search required]
   Search for: pending merger agreements, buyout announcements, tender offers filed in the last
   60 days. An M&A target has an artificially anchored price that removes the model's edge.

4. Material crisis event in the last 30 days  [MANUAL — web search required]
   Search for: SEC/regulatory enforcement actions, bankruptcy filings or liquidity warnings,
   fraud allegations, product safety recalls affecting core revenue, executive departures
   combined with financial restatements. Routine executive changes alone do not qualify.
   Flag only events that materially alter the risk profile over the next 5 trading days.

If MechExclude is True OR you confirm an M&A/crisis event → the ticker is disqualified and drops
out of the ranking pool. No position. Move on.

════════════════════════════════════════════════════════
STEP 2 — RISK FLAGS  (the primary ranking input — these push a candidate DOWN the list)
════════════════════════════════════════════════════════

This is now where most of your judgment goes. For every surviving candidate, tally the flags.
Flags are the main reason one survivor ranks below another. More flags → lower in the queue.

FLAG A: Beta above 1.25
   Beta 1.25–1.5 produced the worst win rate (51.5%) and negative mean PnL. High beta amplifies
   losses faster than gains in a short 2–5 day hold. Check FinViz/Yahoo 5-year monthly beta.

FLAG B: Small cap ($952M–$2.2B) with negative 3-month price performance
   This combination produced a 24.1% BigLoss rate — the worst segment in the dataset.
   Either characteristic alone is acceptable; the combination is not.

FLAG C: Stock trading below its 200-day moving average
   Above SMA200: WR 63.4%, mean PnL 0.77%. Below: WR 52.4%, mean PnL 0.33%.
   The most reliable technical signal in the dataset. Check FinViz SMA200 (negative % = below).

FLAG D: Strong Buy analyst consensus (mean recommendation score below 1.5)
   Counterintuitively the worst band: WR 57.7%, BigLoss 17.5%. Hold-to-Mild-Buy (2.0–3.5)
   produced WR 62–64%, BigLoss 4–6.5%. The bullish thesis is already in the price.
   Check FinViz "Recom". Score < 1.5 triggers this flag.

FLAG E: Debt/Equity above 3.0
   Debt/Eq 1.0–2.0 is the best zone (WR 71.2%, BigLoss 5.8%). Above 3.0, BigLoss rises to 15.9%.
   Note: zero debt is also mildly negative (WR 50%) — do not treat low debt as a positive.

FLAG F: Sector = Real Estate or Consumer Cyclical
   Sharpe: Real Estate 0.055, Consumer Cyclical 0.126 — both trail the 0.30+ of better sectors.
   Not a disqualifier, but requires two positive signals to offset before it ranks well.

FLAG G: 3-month price performance (Perf Quarter) below –5%
   Bottom quartile: WR 55.3%, BigLoss 12.9%, profit factor 1.32. Top quartile (>20%): WR 65.9%,
   BigLoss 4.7%, profit factor 3.09. The strongest standalone risk signal — flag it even without
   the Small-cap combination in Flag B.

════════════════════════════════════════════════════════
STEP 3 — POSITIVE SIGNALS  (the primary tie-breaker — these push a candidate UP the list)
════════════════════════════════════════════════════════

With a surplus of candidates and only 4 slots, positives are now the main tool for choosing
BETWEEN otherwise-clean names. Count them; more positives → higher in the queue.

SIGNAL 1: Mid or Large cap ($2.2B+)
   Mid cap: best mean PnL per trade (0.99%). Large cap: best win rate (63.7%). Together 73% of PnL.

SIGNAL 2: Positive 3-month price performance, especially top quartile (>20%)
   Profit factor 3.09 vs 1.32 for negative Perf Quarter. Single strongest quantitative signal —
   weight this most heavily when ranking.

SIGNAL 3: Beta between 0.5 and 0.75
   Goldilocks zone: WR 65.6%, mean PnL 0.80%, BigLoss 4.9%.

SIGNAL 4: RSI between 50 and 70
   The clean trending zone. RSI 50–60 best mean PnL (0.91%); 60–70 best win rate (62.4%), low
   BigLoss (6.9%).

SIGNAL 5: EPS Q/Q growth above 25%
   >50% quarterly EPS acceleration: WR 64.7%, BigLoss 7.1%. Confirms the directional signal.

SIGNAL 6: Sector = Basic Materials, Healthcare, Consumer Defensive, or Utilities
   Sharpe: Basic Materials 0.526, Healthcare 0.484, Consumer Defensive 0.410. Utilities 90% WR
   (small sample n=10).

SIGNAL 7: Debt/Equity between 0.5 and 2.0
   Sweet spot. WR 59–71%. The 1.0–2.0 band is the best single segment in the dataset (WR 71.2%).

════════════════════════════════════════════════════════
STEP 4 — RANK AND SELECT THE BEST 4
════════════════════════════════════════════════════════

The output is no longer an independent verdict per ticker — it is a RANKED SHORTLIST that fills
the 4-position book. Work in three passes:

PASS 1 — Disqualify.
  Remove every candidate that is hard-excluded:
    • MechExclude == True (automated four), OR
    • a confirmed Active M&A target, OR
    • a confirmed material crisis event.
  These are out. They cannot be ranked back in.

PASS 2 — Tier the survivors.
  For each survivor compute a net quality read = (Step-3 positives) minus (Step-2 flags), then
  assign a tier. Flags carry more weight than positives (asymmetric loss cost), so:
    • TIER 1 (best):     0–1 flags AND ≥2 positives
    • TIER 2 (solid):    0–1 flags AND 0–1 positives
    • TIER 3 (marginal): exactly 2 flags AND ≥1 positive   → only ever ships at REDUCED 50% size
    • DISQUALIFIED:      3+ flags, OR 2 flags with 0 positives

PASS 3 — Select 4.
  Fill the 4 slots from the top down:
    1. Take Tier 1 first, then Tier 2, then (only if still short) Tier 3.
    2. Within a tier, rank by the strongest positives in priority order:
       Signal 2 (top-quartile Perf Quarter) > Signal 4 (RSI 50–70) > Signal 1 (Mid/Large cap)
       > Signal 3 (Beta 0.5–0.75) > the rest. Use lower Step-2 flag count to break further ties,
       then the model's UpProbability as the final tie-break.
    3. Tier-3 names that make the cut ship at 50% size; Tier-1/2 ship at standard size.
    4. List 2 ranked ALTERNATES below the 4 so the broker has a fallback if its intraday
       gap/price filter vetoes one of the top 4 at the open.

  If fewer than 4 names survive Pass 1–2 (thin day): ship only what qualifies. Do NOT reach into
  the disqualified pool to backfill a slot — an empty slot beats a hard-excluded trade. The model
  produces enough candidates that the opportunity cost of an unfilled slot is low; the cost of a
  –4% loss on a 2-day hold is asymmetrically painful.

  If MORE than 4 clean Tier-1 names exist (rich day): that's the good case — take the 4 highest by
  the Pass-3 ranking and keep the rest as alternates.
